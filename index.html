Krishna Rastogi

Hi, I am Krishna Rastogi. Currently, I am a visiting student at Molecular Electronics Lab, Jawaharlal Nehru Centre for Advanced Scientific Research, Bangalore. At, Molecular Electronics lab, I am currentely working on "Object Based Edge Detection Using Single Pixel Organic Polymer/Electrolyte Interfaces"

I was a visiting student in the Camera Culture Group at the MIT Media Lab from April - December, 2014. At the lab, my work focuses on developing novel approaches for low-cost and non-invasive diagnostic medical devices. I am exploring retinal, hair and dental imaging in collaboration with a multidisciplinary team of researchers and scientists.

  • Retinal Imaging: I was developing an imaging set-up to quantitatively analyze retinal and systemic disease manifestations that appear in the retinal blood vessel structure.
  • Hair Imaging: I was establishing an imaging tool that will enable hair cuticle analysis to differentiate between healthy and damaged hair.
  • Dental imaging: I was working on an imaging approach for the oral cavity that could be used on a daily basis and could provide predictive analysis of oral health.

    • Alongside, I am pursuing my undergraduate degree in biotechnology from PES University, Bangalore. During my undergraduate program, I worked on projects involving several disciplines (electronics, environmental, healthcare) to solve real-life problems.

    My research interests are low cost Health Diagnostic, Environmental Studies, Genetic Engineering, Wearables, Mobile-based Technologies, Computational Fluid Dynamics, Optical Engineering, Olfaction Studies and Biomedical Imaging.

    Besides work, I love to travel and admire nature.
